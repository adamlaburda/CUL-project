Post Impressionism

This is a painting by V. Van Gogh named Sunflowers, which is an oil painting.


Origin:

It is very similar to the Impressionist era (it is the continuation to that era). It started in the late 19th century and went on to the 20th century. It retained similar characteristics while having few new.

Ideas & Characteristics:

It was about personal interpretation and conveying artists emotions and deeper meaning rather than the more observational approach of Impressionism. Artists in this movement also more bold and expressive colours with structured, expressive and almost geometric brush strokes.

Artists:

Post Impressionist artists include Gauguin or one of the most popular artists ever - Vincent Van Gogh.



Fauvism

This is a painting from H. Matisse called The Cat with Red Fish, an oil painting.


Origin:

Fauvism was built on Post-Impressionism. It originated in the early 20th century.

Ideas & Characteristics:

As it is a movement build on Post-Impressionism, it shares similarities like expressive lines. However, there are several new characteristics that include vivid and unmixed colour and bold approach (e.g.: sky could be orange). Fauvism aimed at conveying emotions through colour and form rather than with a realistic representation of reality.

Artists:

Example of a Fauvist artist is Henri Matisse.
