Impressionism

This is a picture called Woman with a Parasol by Monet, an oil painting.


Origin:

French modern art movement that emerged in the second half of the 19th century (1862). It marked a departure from traditional artistic techniques and embraced a more innovative and subjective approach. It ended around 1920.

Ideas & Characteristics:

Impressionism was focused on more subjective perspective and visual perception. Impressionist artists embraced this innovative approach by using new techniques to capture light, movement, atmosphere, etc. These included techniques such as loosening their brush work, which meant thin and precise strokes, and with using a lighter palettes with pure colours. Other characteristics of Impressionism include capturing the moment, meaning everyday scenes, and a play with asymmetry.
