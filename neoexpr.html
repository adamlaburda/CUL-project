Neo-Expressionism

This is the artwork from Georg Baselitz titled “Rebel”, in which you can see the bold stroke emphasizing the personal perspective and emotions.


Origin:

Emerged in late 20th century. It was a revival of the expressive and emotionally charged artworks, in contrast to minimalism or conceptual art which abandoned the emotive aspect of art. It rejected these movements (ex.: Minimalism).

Ideas & Characteristics:

It was the revival of the Expressionism movement from the early 20th century, meaning that there are several similarities between them. To name a few, both movements put a large emphasis on the emotional intensity. They also share the characteristic bold, expressive brushstrokes that emphasize the subjective and emotional perspective. It used figurative imagery and symbolism, which means that is incorporated recognizable forms of images and symbolism helped them convey strong themes and personal messages.

Artists:

Artists include Julian Schnabel or Georg Baselitz.
