Origin:

This art movement emerged in mid 1950s during the post-WWII consumer commodity boom. They gained popularity especially in United States and the United Kingdom. Reaction against the perceived elitism of traditional. It was influenced by popular culture. It is a departure from Abstract Expressionism

Ideas & Characteristics:

Artists of this era celebrated the popular culture and expressed it in their paintings. They are used imagery from mass media like advertisements, popular icons blurring the line between art and commerce. This movement is characterized by the use of bold, vibrant colour and repeated imagery to create visually appealing recognizable art. Some art also contains elements of irony and critique, questioning the commodification of art and popular culture.

Artists:

Artists include Andy Warhol, Roy Lichtenstein, Claes Oldenburg or Richard Hamilton.
