Cubism

This is an artwork from Pablo Picasso called “Girl with mandolin” from 1910 that clearly showcases the ideas of cubism.


Origin:

Cubism originated in 20th century. It was, most notably, pioneered by Pablo Picasso and Braque. It marked a radical departure from traditional artistic representation. It was an abstract art movement. It was influenced by African and Iberian art.

Ideas & Characteristics:

It was supposed to be a new way of describing space and volume and mass. It abandoned perspective and realism (realistic modeling). It is characterized by its use of geometry, and several viewpoints and facets of the objects, offering viewers a more comprehensive understanding of the subject. It was usually more focused on the structure and form than on a large colour palette.

Artists:

As mentioned above, most popular artists of cubism are Pablo Picasso and Braque.
