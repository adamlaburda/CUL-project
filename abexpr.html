Abstract Expressionism

This is a painting by Jackson Pollock named ‘Number 1A 1948’, which clearly showcases his ‘poured’ technique in Action paintings.


Origin:

Abstract Expressionism began in mid 20th century primarily in New York. It was a post-war art.

Ideas & Characteristics:

It is characterized by its use of gestural abstraction. It took the idea of exploring the unconscious mind from Surrealism while also taking some ideas from expressionism, like the freedom of self expression. The art were large scale and tried to make an immersive experience for the audience. Some artists of this movement used specific colours to express specific emotions. The term ‘Action painting’ was created in this era. It is used to describe the dynamic, spontaneous ‘poured’ approach some artists,like Jackson Pollock, used.

Artists:

Some artists of Abstract Expressionism are Jackson Pollock, Willem de Kooning or Mark Rothko.
