Dada

This is an artwork by Hannah Höch named “Words of Wisdom”, a collage.


Origin:

Dadaism emerged during World War I, originating in Zurich in the early 20th century. It was a response to the absurdity of war and societal conventions.

Ideas & Characteristics:

It was an anti-war movement. It portrayed the absurdity and nihilism. It advocated for more anarchic and free-spirited approach with an unconventional and thought-provoking composition, achieved by its unique techniques of assemblage and collaging. It frequently included some sort of political or social critique.

Artists:

Examples of artists are Marcel Duchamp and Hannah Höch.



Surrealism

This is an artwork by Rene Magritte named “The Pleasure Principle”


Origin:

Surrealism emerged in the 20th century. It was heavily influenced by Dada movement. Surrealism was influenced by Freudian psychoanalysis, particularly the idea of the unconscious mind and the exploration of repressed desires.

Ideas & Characteristics:

It wanted to explore the unconscious mind, drawing inspiration from dreams, fantasies and the irrational. This is achieved through a heavy use of symbolism, imagery and unexpected combinations of forms. Surrealism seeks to break free from the constraints of logical thought.

Artists:

Examples of artist from this era are Salvador Dalí or René Magritte.
