Expressionism

This is a artwork entitled ‘Degenerate artist’, which is a self-portrait of Oskar Kokoschka.


Origin:

Emerged in early 20th century in cities across Germany and later spread to the rest of Europe. It was a reaction to Impressionism and Post-Impressionism. It was a response to a fear of loss of authenticity.

Ideas & Characteristics:

The focus of expressionism was on portraying painters feelings and emotions. It shows a subjective perspective of the author. Artists used bold and dynamic brushwork, sometimes creating textured surfaces. This added to the overall emotional impact of the artworks. Expressionists employed bold and vivid colors to convey mood and emotion.

Artists:

Examples of expressionist artists include Oskar Kokoschka, Edvard Munch, the author of ‘The Scream’, and Van Gogh.
